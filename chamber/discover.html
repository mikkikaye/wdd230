An old linden tree (Tilia) growing in town in 1901 inspired the present (misspelled) name.

Residents used 1.1 billion gallons of secondary water last year.

25% of Lindon residents have a Bachelor's Degree.

Lindon owns and operates 6 snowplows.

The median household income is $100,351.

Originally settled in 1861, Lindon began as pioneers moved into what was then the Lindon grazing land. The town was originally named "String Town" because of the way the houses were strung up and down the street between the towns of Orem and Pleasant Grove.

Lindon's elevation is 4,642 ft above sea level.

2018: Best Places in Utah to Raise a Family & 2017: Safest Cities in the State of Utah

With more than 34 trails covering 272 miles you're bound to find a perfect trail for you.

183 veterans call Lindon their home.

8 tons of asphalt was used to fill potholes last year.
